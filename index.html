When do I use an apostrophe?

  • Use an apostrophe for contractions. Examples:
    • "don't" is a contraction of "do not"
    • "it's" is a contraction of "it is"
    • "let's" is a contraction of "let us"
  • Use an apostrophe for possessives of nouns, but not pronouns. Examples:
    • "John's thing" means "thing" belongs to "John"
    • "its thing" (*no apostrophe* because "it" is a pronoun) means "thing" belongs to "it"
